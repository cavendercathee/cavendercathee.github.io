Biography

Ike Jones was born in California on December 23, 1929. Producer of the 1966 film A Man Called Adam, he was the first African-American to produce a major Hollywood movie. He also became famous for his secret marriage to film star Inger Stevens. His first production, A Man Called Adam, starred Sammy Davis, Jr. He was a star football player at the University of California-Los Angeles and was drafted by the Green Bay Packers following his graduation.
