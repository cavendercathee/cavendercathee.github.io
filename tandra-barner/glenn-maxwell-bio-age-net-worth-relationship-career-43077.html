Who is Glenn Maxwell?

Glenn Maxwell is a cricketer from Australia who possesses exceptional fielding, bowling, and devastating power as a batsman. The all-arounder represents Australia in the Twenty20, One Day International (ODI), and Test formats.

In the realm of Australian domestic cricket, he is a member of the ‘Melbourne Stars’ and ‘Victoria.’ Previously, he represented the Delhi Daredevils (now Delhi Capitals), Mumbai Indians, and Kings XI Punjab in the ‘Indian Premier League (IPL).’ “Lancashire County Cricket Club” is his representative in English county cricket.

He bats with his right hand and employs an off-break bowling technique with his right arm. He began his cricketing career as a batsman for a local juvenile club before progressing to pace and spin bowler and eventually becoming a proficient fielder. His propensity to execute high-risk shots with evident ease earned him the moniker “The Big Show” from his teammates.

This trait is said to have caused considerable concern among fielding captains. Additionally, he holds several records and has been honored with multiple ‘Man of the Match’ accolades. He maintains an exceptional level of performance in English amateur cricket, as well as in domestic and international matches for Australia.

Where was Glenn Maxwell born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

On October 14, 1988, Glenn James Maxwell was born in Kew, Victoria, Australia, to Neil Maxwell, his father, and Joy Maxwell, his mother. Daniel Maxwell, his older brother, is another sibling.

During his elementary school years, he was compelled to bat left-handed due to his exceptional skill and the inability of his peers to bowl him out. He commenced his juvenile cricket career with the Victorian “South Belgrave Cricket Club” at a tender age.

How did Glenn Maxwell start his Professional Career?

Glenn Maxwell commenced his professional cricket career in July 2009 upon his selection for the ‘Victoria’ state team. For the Australian Institute of Sports, he participated in the ‘Hong Kong Cricket Sixes’ and the ‘Emerging Players Tournament’ in January 2010.

He made his first-class debut in February 2011 for ‘Victoria’ against ‘NSW.’ During the ‘Ryobi One-Day Cup,’ he achieved the fastest fifty in Australian domestic One-Day cricket, catapulting him to instantaneous fame.

He played club cricket for the English teams ‘South Wilts’ and ‘Hampshire Second XI’ in addition to two IPL matches for ‘Delhi Daredevils’ in 2012. Amidst Australia’s tour of the United Arab Emirates, he debuted in one ODI against Afghanistan and one T20I against Pakistan. In the same year, he also participated in the ICC World Twenty20.

He made his test debut against India in March 2013. The same year, he joined the ‘Mumbai Indians for the IPL and participated in the ‘2013 ICC Champions Trophy’ as well.

He contributed significantly to his IPL team, Kings XI Punjab, in 2014. The ‘2014 ICC World Twenty20’ witnessed him maintain his outstanding form. His form plummeted during a test series against Pakistan in the UAE, but his fortunes improved in the One-Day Internationals.

Despite his poor performance in 2015, he was named to Australia’s ‘2015 ICC Cricket World Cup squad, where his explosive batting raised eyebrows.

At the SCG, he defeated Sri Lanka with a century off 51 balls during the World Cup; it is the second-fastest century in World Cup history and the quickest by an Australian in one-day internationals. Nevertheless, he was cut from ‘Kings XI Punjab that year due to his lack of success in the IPL.

After playing club cricket in England for some time, Maxwell participated in numerous international tournaments during the 2015-16 season, including ODIs against India, New Zealand, West Indies, and South Africa.

Following his absence from the Test and ODI matches during Australia’s 2016 tour of Sri Lanka, he defeated Sri Lanka in a Twenty20 International by amassing an unbeaten 145 off 65 deliveries.

Amidst match-fixing allegations, he scored his first-ever test century against India in Ranchi in March 2017. Moreover, despite being appointed captain of “Kings XI Punjab” for the 2017 IPL season, he was unsuccessful.

Throughout the 2017-18 season, he contributed to Australia’s victory in the ‘Trans-Tasman Tri-Series’ and delivered an exceptional performance in the ‘Big Bash League’ (BBL).

In 2017, he also achieved his first double-century in the Sheffield Shield competition. For USD 1.75 million, the ‘Delhi Daredevils’ acquired him for the 2018 IPL season; however, he failed to deliver. ‘Cricket Australia’ awarded him a national contract for the 2018-19 season in April.

Under his leadership, Australia decisively defeated England in the ‘2018 Tri-Series.’ After allegations of match-fixing in 2017 resurfaced, he was dropped from Australia’s test tour selection for the UAE in 2018 but was called in for the Twenty20 matches.

Against India in February 2019, his knockout century and scintillating innings led Australia to an astounding Twenty20 victory. He performed exceptionally well during the March 2019 Pakistan vs. Australia ODI series in the UAE.

Glenn was allowed to represent Australia in the ‘2019 Cricket World Cup squad in April 2019. He is a member of the ‘Melbourne Stars’ in the Australian Twenty20 competition, the ‘Lancashire County Cricket Club’ of England, and the ‘Victoria’ first-class cricket team at present.

Honors and Accomplishments

  • Glenn Maxwell received the prestigious ‘Player of the Tournament accolade at the ‘Hong Kong Cricket Sixes tournament in 2010.
    He scored fifty in the ‘Ryobi One-Day Cup’ in 2011 to become the quickest domestic achiever in Australia’s history.
  • During a 2013 match against India, he established the joint-fastest 50-run record for Australia in One-Day Internationals. He was named ‘Man of the Match’ four times throughout the 2014 IPL season.
  • He established the records for the “fastest ODI century by an Australian” and the “second-fastest century in World Cup history” during the “2015 ICC Cricket World Cup.” The ‘Australian Twenty20 International Player of the Year’ accolade was also bestowed upon him during that particular year.
  • During Australia’s 2016 tour of Sri Lanka, he amassed the second-highest individual score in Twenty20 International history. He became the first player to make a century as an opening batsman in his debut tie. In the same year, he was also named the “Australian ODI Player of the Year.”
  • In 2017, he made history in Ranchi during a test match against India by becoming the second Australian batsman to achieve centuries in all three formats of cricket. He joined an elite group of cricketers who had accomplished this feat at some point in their careers.
  • During Australia’s Twenty20 tour of India in February 2019, he established a world record by becoming the third cricketer to achieve three centuries in the Twenty20 format. Additionally, within the same series, he was named “Man of the Match” and “Man of the Series.”
