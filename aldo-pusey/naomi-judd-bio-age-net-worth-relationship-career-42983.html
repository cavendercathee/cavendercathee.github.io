How did Naomi Judd start her Professional Career?

Judd relocated to Tennessee in the late 1970s and began performing alongside Wynonna. The group was contracted by Joe Galante, the Executive Chairman of RCA, after their appearance on “The Ralph Emery Show” in 1983. “Mama He’s Crazy,” which appeared on the Judds’ gold EP “Wynonna & Naomi,” was awarded their inaugural Grammy in 1983.

“Rockin’ with the Rhythm” was an album that Clan Judd achieved platinum certification with the number-one singles “Have Mercy,” “Grandpa (Tell Me About the Good Old Days),” and “Rockin’ with the Rhythm of the Rain.” It also reached its highest position on the list of Top Country Albums.

On the Top Country Albums chart, the platinum-certified “Christmas Time with The Judds” from 1987 was the first album by The Judds to fail to reach the number one position, peaking at #49. “River of Time,” a gold-certified follow-up album to their holiday album that the Judds released in 1989, peaked at number two on the Top Country Albums chart.

She served as a mentor and adjudicator on the CMT competition program “Can You Duet” in 2008. In 2011, she co-starred with Wynonna on the OWN: Oprah Winfrey Network reality series “The Judds.”

“Back to the Well” was the Judds’ 2017 reunion performance at Bridgestone Arena in Nashville, which coincided with Kenny Rogers’ final performance, “All in for the Gambler.”

In addition, their performances earned them nine Country Music Association Awards, including the Horizon Award in 1984, the Single of the Year award in 1985 for “Why Not Me,” and the Vocal Group award in 1986. Naomi was honored with the Golden Plate Award from the American Academy of Achievements in 2013.
